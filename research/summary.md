# Therapist Website Research Pack

## Brief Snapshot
- Practice: Rehab's private counselling practice launch.
- Audience: adults and couples seeking safe, supportive telehealth counselling in Australia.
- Required tone: calm, professional, inclusive, heartwarming.
- Core palette from brief: sage green, off-white, beige, soft pink, muted gold.
- Core site structure from brief: Home, About, Services, plus clear booking/contact flow.

## Design Patterns Observed
- Therapist websites that feel credible usually lead with either a therapist portrait or a highly personal environment, not abstract wellness imagery alone.
- The strongest examples use one primary CTA and keep the booking language direct but gentle.
- Service sections work best when grouped into simple categories instead of dense paragraphs.
- Trust signals are lightweight: qualifications, registration, telehealth availability, and a warm personal introduction.
- The market leans toward calm neutrals and editorial photography, but the best examples avoid looking like spa brands.
- Booking UX should feel separate from the main brand mood. The visual tone can stay warm while the booking step becomes more structured and explicit.

## Concept Directions

### Warm Editorial
- Best match for the brief.
- Uses soft interior photography, tactile textures, warm daylight, and emotional calm.
- Feels personal and trustworthy without becoming overly soft or vague.
- Best suited for Home and About pages.

- `concept-images/warm-editorial-hero-v1.png`
- `concept-images/warm-editorial-supporting-v1.png`

### Calm Clinical
- Cleanest option for signaling professionalism and telehealth readiness.
- Uses lighter structure, subtle desk/workspace cues, and more breathing room.
- Works well if the final brand should feel especially clear and organised.

Files:
- `concept-images/calm-clinical-hero-v1.png`

### Community Trust
- Strongest option for inclusion, warmth, and relational safety.
- More human and outward-facing than the other two directions.
- Best used as a secondary/supporting visual language rather than the sole homepage style.

Files:
- `concept-images/community-trust-hero-v1.png`

## Recommendation
Recommend `Warm Editorial` as the primary direction.

Why it fits best:
- It aligns most closely with the PDF brief's language of calm, heartwarming, and inclusive care.
- It supports a premium but approachable first impression for adults and couples.
- It leaves room to borrow the structural clarity of `Atenta` without inheriting its more severe visual tone.
- It can incorporate selective `Community Trust` imagery later for About or Services sections, especially when signaling inclusion and relational warmth.

## Suggested Next Build Direction
- Home hero: use the `Warm Editorial` interior style with left-aligned copy and a single primary booking CTA.
- About section: combine a real portrait of Rehab with the `Warm Editorial` palette and sparse trust details.
- Services section: use the `Atenta` style of clear grouped links or cards rather than long descriptive blocks.
- Booking page: follow the clarity of the Counselling in Melbourne example, with telehealth clearly surfaced early.
- Trust strip: include ACA registration, trauma-informed approach, languages spoken, and Australia-wide telehealth availability.
